PROTOCOL EXECUTION:
HOW TO READ THE GRID

THE GEAR

Two moderately heavy dumbbells and a floor. No hyper-specialized machines. Keep it primal.

THE PHASES

WEEK 1: THE FOUNDATION. Form over ego. Establish the baseline. Wake up the muscles.

WEEK 2: THE FIRE. Increased weight. Decreased rest. Your lungs will burn.

WEEK 3: THE FORGE. Extreme high volume. Severe conditioning. Psychological warfare.

WEEK 4: THE RECKONING. Maximum intensity. AMRAP protocols. Leave nothing in the tank.

EXECUTION STANDARDS

When the table dictates a time block (e.g., 45s), you work for maximum reps (AMRAP) within that window before resting. Do not drop the iron until the clock stops. "Failure" means your form broke entirely, not that it "started getting hard". Push past the boundary.

04

PRE-FORGE:
NEURAL ACTIVATION

Going into these circuits cold is a guarantee for injury and suboptimal performance. You must prime the central nervous system, increase synovial fluid in the joints, and spike your core temperature. This sequence is mandatory before every single workout. No exceptions.

EXECUTE 3 ROUNDS IN UNBROKEN SUCCESSION:

  • JUMPING JACKS: 50 Reps — Get the heart rate spiking.
  • WALKOUT TO PLANK: 10 Reps — Activate the core and stretch the hamstrings.
  • SPIDERMAN LUNGES: 10 Reps per leg — Open up the hips and thoracic spine violently.
  • AIR SQUATS: 20 Reps — Drive knees out, chest up.
  • ARM CIRCLES: 15 Reps — Small to large, forward and backward. Prep the rotators.

Once complete, your lungs should be open and you should be lightly sweating. Pick up the dumbbells and initiate the day's protocol.

05
THE ENGINE ROOM

NUTRITIONAL PROTOCOL:
THE FUEL

If you execute this training perfectly but eat like a child, you will fail. The Shred Protocol is not magic; it relies on strict energy balance and macronutrient optimization. Your body is a machine. Stop putting cheap fuel in it.

1. CALORIC DEFICIT (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Take your goal bodyweight in pounds and multiply by 12. That is your daily caloric ceiling. If you want to weigh 180 lbs, you eat 2160 calories. No cheating. Every bite counts.

2. PROTEIN: THE BUILDING BLOCK

You will consume 1 gram of protein per pound of goal bodyweight every single day. This is what prevents muscle catabolism while you are burning fat. Lean meats, eggs, low-fat dairy.

3. CARBOHYDRATES & FATS

Carbohydrates are energy for the heavy lifting. Fats regulate hormones. Keep carbs around your workout window (pre and post-workout). Avoid heavy carbs right before bed. Keep dietary fats moderate (nuts, olive oil, avocado).

4. HYDRATION STANDARD

Drink one full gallon (3.7 liters) of water daily. It flushes metabolic waste, aids recovery, and suppresses artificial hunger signals. Black coffee and green tea are approved; sugary drinks are poison.

06

FIELD RATIONS:
BATTLE-TESTED MEALS

Do not overcomplicate your diet. Complexity breeds excuses. Build a routine of 3-4 trusted meals and rotate them ruthlessly. Food is not entertainment; it is fuel. Here are your default rations.

BREAKFAST INITIATION

  • THE ANVIL: 3 whole eggs, 3 egg whites, 1 cup dry oats cooked with cinnamon and blueberries. (High protein, slow-release carbs).
  • THE FASTED: Black coffee, water, and pure hatred. Do not eat until lunch if utilizing Intermittent Fasting routines to extend the fat-burning window.

LUNCH / MID-DAY REFUEL

  • THE ENGINE: 200g grilled chicken breast, 1 cup jasmine rice, massive serving of steamed broccoli. Basic, effective, repeatable.
  • THE CARNIVORE BOWL: 250g lean ground beef (93/7), half an avocado, bell peppers, hot sauce. Zero carbs, high satiety.

DINNER / RECOVERY

  • THE OMEGA: 200g wild caught salmon fillet, 1 large baked sweet potato, asparagus roasted in 1 tbsp olive oil. Elite recovery fuel.
  • THE STANDARD: Lean sirloin steak, massive side salad loaded with leafy greens, cucumber, and a light vinaigrette.

PHASE 1 — THE FOUNDATION

DAY 01: FULL BODY STRENGTH

Establish the baseline. Control the eccentric.

Day 1 sets the tone for the entire month. You leave your excuses at the door today. We initiate with compound movements to wake up the central nervous system. Goblet squats will build the base; keep your chest up, drive through your heels, and do not let your knees cave. The floor press will attack your chest and triceps while protecting your shoulders. Focus heavily on the eccentric (lowering) phase. If it's not burning, your dumbbells are too light.

Movement Sets Reps Rest
Goblet Squats 4 12 60s
DB Floor Press 4 12 60s
Bent-Over Rows 4 12 60s
DB Reverse Lunges 3 10/leg 60s
FINISHER: The Death Hang. Hang from a pull-up bar until grip failure. Repeat 3x.
08

DAY 02: UPPER HIIT

Cardiovascular expansion. Max reps under tension.

Today we introduce metabolic stress. This isn't a relaxed bodybuilding pump session. The 40-second block means you do not drop the weights until the clock stops. Your back will burn on the Renegade Rows, but you must lock your core—do not let your hips twist. When the timer hits the Burbees, turn your brain off and just move. The goal here is gasping for air while demanding your upper body to continue firing.

Movement Sets Reps Rest
Renegade Rows 4 40s (Max Reps) 20s
DB Push Press 4 40s (Max Reps) 20s
Pushups 4 40s (Max Reps) 20s
Burpees 4 40s (Max Reps) 20s
FINISHER: 50 Crunches. Unbroken.
09

DAY 03: LOWER FATIGUE

Leg destruction. Drive through the heels.

Your legs are the largest muscle group in your body. Training them requires massive oxygen output and causes immense caloric burn. The DB Romanian Deadlift is about feeling a stretch in the hamstrings; push your hips back like you are closing a door with your glutes. When you transition to Jump Squats, you will want to quit. Do not. Explode upward and land softly. Embrace the lactic acid pooling in your quads.

Movement Sets Reps Rest
DB Romanian Deadlifts 4 15 60s
DB Front Squats 4 12 60s
Jump Squats 4 20 60s
FINISHER: Hollow Body Hold. 60 seconds × 3 sets.
10

DAY 04: CORE & CARRY

Midline stability and grip strength.

A weak core equates to a weak body. Your spine needs armor. The Heavy Farmer's Walk is a full-body movement masquerading as grip work; squeeze the dumbbells until your knuckles turn white, brace your abs, and march with perfect posture. If the weight isn't trying to pull your shoulders out of their sockets, you went too light. The Russian Twists must be deliberate—no sloppy momentum.

Movement Sets Reps Rest
Heavy Farmer's Walk 4 60s (Max Distance) 30s
Russian Twists 4 45s (Max Reps) 15s
Plank Hold 4 60s (Hold) 30s
FINISHER: Max effort pushups in 2 minutes. Stop only when your chest gives out.

DAY 05: FULL BODY METCON

High-intensity metabolic conditioning. Gas pedal down.

Metcon (Metabolic Conditioning) is where fat perishes. Thrusters are arguably the most taxing movement you can perform with dumbbells. A full squat straight into a violent overhead press. You will do this for 45 seconds straight, rest a meager 15 seconds, and go right into snatches. The rest period isn't designed for recovery; it is designed merely so you don't pass out. Keep the gas pedal pressed firmly to the floor.

Movement Sets Reps Rest
DB Thrusters 5 45s (Max Reps) 15s
Alt DB Snatches 5 45s (Max Reps) 15s
Mountain Climbers 5 45s (Max Reps) 15s
FINISHER: 30 Jump Squats.

DAY 06 & 07:
THE GRIND & RECOVERY

DAY 06: THE GRIND

The ultimate punishment. 100 Man-Makers for Time.

This is a psychological test. The movement: Pushup → Right arm row → Left arm row → Jump feet forward → Stand up into a Push Press. That is ONE rep. Execute 100 as fast as humanly possible. You will want to quit at 30. Your lungs will burn at 50. Keep going. Document your time.

DAY 07: MANDATORY RECOVERY

You survived Week 1, but the real work has barely begun. Eat a high-protein diet today to repair the muscle tearing from yesterday. Drink a full gallon of water. Walk outside for 45 minutes to flush the lactic acid from your legs. Sleep at least 9 hours. You will need it for Phase 2.

PHASE 2 — THE FIRE

DAY 08: PUSH HEAVY

Rest is reduced to 45s. Add 5 lbs to your dumbbells if applicable.

Phase 2 tightens the screws. By reducing the rest period to 45 seconds, we are forcing your body to recover faster under a heavier load. This is a pure push day. On the DB Floor Press, completely stop the momentum at the bottom by letting your triceps rest on the floor for a microsecond before exploding upward. The flyes should stretch the pectorals violently.

Movement Sets Reps Rest
DB Floor Press 5 10 45s
DB Chest Flyes 4 12 45s
DB Clean & Press 4 10 45s
FINISHER: Pushups to failure × 3 sets. Break the 90-degree plane on every rep.

DAY 09: PULL HEAVY

Back width and bicep thickness. Squeeze the lats.

You cannot build an imposing physique without a massive back. Today focuses strictly on pulling mechanics. On the Bent-Over Rows, keep your back completely flat—parallel to the floor. Pull the dumbbells to your hips, not your chest. For the pullovers, lie on the floor or a bench, utilizing a single heavy dumbbell. Stretch the lats out maximally at the bottom of the movement. Build the armor.

Movement Sets Reps Rest
DB Bent-Over Rows 5 10 45s
DB Pullovers 4 12 45s
Alt DB Curls 4 10/arm 45s
FINISHER: The Death Hang. 3× to failure. Your forearms must scream.

DAY 10: LOWER HIIT

Lactic acid threshold. Do not stop moving.

Welcome to the crucible. Lower body HIIT is notoriously unpleasant because of the immense cardiovascular demand. DB Front Squats force your core to fire to keep the weight from collapsing your chest. The DB Swings must be powered 100% by the hips—thrust violently forward and let the dumbbells float. Broad Jumps demand explosive power when you are already fatigued. Fight through it.

Movement Sets Reps Rest
DB Front Squats 5 45s (Max Reps) 15s
DB Swings 5 45s (Max Reps) 15s

Shoulder and chest destruction under the clock.

Do not lock out your elbows completely on the push press; keep the deltoids under constant strain. Use your legs for a slight dip and drive on the press. Push the pace.

Movement Sets Reps Rest
Renegade Rows 4 45s (Max Reps) 15s

DAY 12: THE GAUNTLET

Pure survival. 50s of work is brutal. Breathe when you can.

The Gauntlet removes the illusion that you are in control. 50 seconds of work with only 10 seconds of rest means you barely have time to take two deep breaths before initiating the next set. Thrusters combined with Burpees will absolutely redline your heart rate. This day is about pure, unadulterated grit. When the final buzzer sounds, you should have nothing left to give.

Movement Sets Reps Rest
DB Thrusters 4 50s (Max Reps) 10s
Burpees 4 50s (Max Reps) 10s
Jump Squats 4 50s (Max Reps) 10s

Bulgarian Split Squats are widely considered the most painful leg exercise in existence; keep your chest up and drive through your front heel. The DB Deadlifts should scorch your hamstrings.

Isolate the side deltoids perfectly on the lateral raises.

NO EXCUSES:
PROTOCOL FAQ

Q: I missed a day. Do I skip it or make it up?
You make it up. You do not skip days. Hold yourself accountable. If you skip, the entire protocol is void.
Q: Can I swap an exercise if I don't like it?
No. You don't skip an exercise because you don't like it; you do it precisely because it is hard. The movements you hate are the ones you need the most.
Q: I'm incredibly sore. Should I train?
Yes. Blood flow from training will help flush the lactic acid. Warm up thoroughly and get to work. Soreness is weakness leaving the body.
